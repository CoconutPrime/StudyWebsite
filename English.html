10th Grade Study Guide: English

SWBAT have an excellent understanding of puncuations and their rules.



Commas are a punctuation mark which are meant to be a pause between parts of a sentence, It can also be used to separate items in a list, and to mark the place in large number.

Rule 1. Use commas to separate items in a list of three or more items

  • Mandy had to finish her homework, cook, and complete her chores.
  • Ally went to the mall with Sam,Mason, and Ariel.

Rule 2. Use commas between coordinate adjectives

  • We saw a happy lively pug.
  • We saw a young golden retriever.

First, try to add and between the 2 coordinate adjectives

  • We saw a happy and lively pug.
  • We saw a young and golden retriever.

Second, try reversing the adjectives around

  • We saw a lively happy pug.
  • We saw a golden young retriever(This does not make sense when it is reversed, so it does not need a comma).

Third, if the sentence still makes sense, then separate it by a comma

  • We saw a happy,lively pug.
  • We saw a young golden retriever.

Rule 3. Use commas in a compound sentence; before FANBOYS

  • Lauren read the paper, but she did not agree with its claim.
  • Maya had worked overnight, yet she completed her homework.

Rule 4. Use commas with Introductory words

  • After it had rained, Emma had went to play outside.
  • During the lesson, you can write notes.

Rule 5. Use commas with nonessential words, phrases, and clauses

  • However, many people think that heroes are born not made.
  • However, Mrs.Addison was wrong.

Semicolons are a punctuation mark (;) which are meant to be a pause, between two main clauses.

  • I am here, you are over there.
  • The monkey is purple, it is strange.

Colon is a punctuation mark (:) which is used when it is introducing a quotation or a list of items or to separate two clauses.

  • The school specializes in four subjects: engineer, architecture, psychology, and computer programing.
  • Julius Caesar spoke disappointed to Brutus: Et tu, Brute?

Quotation Marks are a set of punctuation marks, they are used to mark the beginning and end of a title or quote.

  • My teacher said make sure to study for the finals.
  • My mother always told me don’t talk to strangers.

Parentheses and Brackets are a set of punctuation marks that are used to enclose explanatory or qualifying words.

  • Earlier this month(April 1), molly had broke her nose.
  • She had got an eighty (80) on her Chemistry test.

Apostrophes are a set of punctuation marks that are used to show claim/owner-shit or the omission of letters or numbers.

  • Ally's pen was stolen.
  • My aunt had grauated college in 08'.

Hyphens are a the sign (-) that is used to join words together.

  • I am looking for a short-term job.
  • Jane was sitting next to my red-haired cousin,James.

Dashes indicate add emphasis, an interruption.

  • I wish you would—oh, never mind.
  • I pay the bills—she has all the fun.

Ellipses is a punctuation mark consisting of three dots.

  • Juliet had said where ought thou romeo...
  • In the Declaration Of Independence, it stated We the people...

Question Marks is a punctuation mark indicating a question.

  • Did you finish your homework?
  • Can you please pass the gravy?

Explination Points is a punctuation mark indicating emotion,suprisement.

  • Yay! We won!
  • Abby screamed Yay! I am going to Europe!

Periods is a punctuation mark indicating the sentence is over.

  • Today we are going to the mall.
  • I can not wait till it is Friday.
