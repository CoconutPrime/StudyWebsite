10th Grade Study Guide: History

SWBAT have a clear understanding of the history throughout the world.



The Paleolithic Era

  • Stone Age: Tools made out of stone.
  • Nomadic: People had no permanent settlement, followed their prey.
  • People made clothing and shelter.

The Neolithic Revolution

  • "New" Stone Age: Hence the word "Neo".
  • Approximately during 3000 B.C.E.
  • Domestication: Trained animals and planted crops.
  • Non Nomadic: Had permanent settlements.

Indus River Valley

  • Permament settlement.
  • Located by Pakistan and Western India.
  • Very peaceful settlement.

Mesopotamia

  • Mesopotamia:Greeek Meaning "between 2 rivers"
  • In modern day Iraq
  • 1992 CE wheel was invented

Ancient China

  • Oldest and longest civilizations in the history of the world
  • Can be traced back to 4,000 years.
  • Located on eastern Asia.

Ancient Greece

  • Traced back to Stone Age hunters.

Ancient Rome

  • Beginning in the eighth century B.C.

Dark Ages

  • Time Period:1100 - 750 BC
  • The period between the fall of the Mycenean civilizations
  • the readoption of writing in the eigth or seventh century BC.

Byzantine Empire

  • Can be traced to 330 A.D.
  • the western half of the Roman Empire crumbled and fell in 476.

The Crusades

  • The first of the Crusades began in 1095

The Ottoman Empire

  • Ottoman Empire was empire created by Turkish tribes in Anatolia
  • The Ottoman period spanned more than 600 years and came to an end in 1922
  • it was replaced by the Turkish Republic and various successor states in southeastern Europe and the Middle East.

The Renaissance

  • The renassance: rebirth,
  • European civilization immediately following the Middle Ages

The Colombian Exchange

  • Refers to a period of cultural and biological exchanges between the New and Old Worlds.
  • Exchanged: plants, animals, diseases and technology

Protestant Reformation

  • 16th-century religious, political, intellectual and cultural upheaval that splintered Catholic Europe
  • setting in place the structures and beliefs that would define the continent in the modern era.

Mexican Revolution

  • 1910, people in Mexico were discontented.
  • Three-fifths of the population were Indian, and they had been losing traditional lands to whites.

Treaty Of Versailles

  • Republicans in November 1918 captured both the US House of Representatives and the Senate
  • US public favored a rapid return home of American troops in Europe and the hanging of Germany's former king

World War I

  • late June 1914, Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria was assassinated by a Serbian nationalist in Sarajevo, Bosnia
  • Lead to: An escalation of threats and mobilization orders followed the incident
  • Central Powers:Germany, Austria-Hungary and the Ottoman Empire
  • Allied Powers: Great Britain, France, Russia, Italy and Japan

The Holocaust

  • The word Holocaust, from the Greek words holos (whole)
  • Historically used to describe a sacrificial offering burned on an altar
  • The mass murder of some 6 million European Jews
